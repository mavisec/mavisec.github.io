🔐 What is CompTIA Security+?

CompTIA Security+ is a globally recognized entry-level certification that validates your understanding of essential cybersecurity concepts. It’s often the first cert people earn when starting their careers in IT or cybersecurity. Many employers (including government jobs) accept Security+ as a baseline requirement.

Exam Code: SY0-701 (launched in Nov 2023)
Duration: 90 minutes
Questions: Up to 90
Question Types: Multiple choice + performance-based
Passing Score: 750/900
Price: ~$392 USD (discount vouchers available online)


🧠 SY0-701: Updated Exam Domains

The latest version (SY0-701) is more streamlined than SY0-601, now with 5 main domains:

  • General Security Concepts (12%)

  • Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Mitigations (22%)

  • Security Architecture (18%)

  • Security Operations (28%)

  • Security Program Management & Oversight (20%)

It focuses more on foundational understanding, risk, cloud security, and practical defensive knowledge than memorizing tools.


📅 My 22-Day Study Plan (Feb 16 – Mar 9)

I had a short timeline, so I stuck to one rule: one great course + one strong question bank = pass.

Week 1 (Feb 16 – Feb 28): The Foundation

  • Watched Andrew Ramdayal’s Full Security+ (SY0-701) Course

  • Took detailed notes daily (3–4 hours/day)

  • Focused on understanding concepts, not just remembering acronyms

  • Completed the labs where needed

Week 2 (Feb 28 – Mar 6): Practice Questions + PBQs

  • Switched to Crucial Exams (crucialexams.com)

  • Reviewed weak areas by rewatching select video segments

Week 3 (Mar 6 – Mar 8): Mock Tests + Final Review

  • Focused entirely on retesting incorrect questions

  • Built a personal cheat sheet for last-minute review

  • Did full-length mock tests under timed conditions

March 9: Passed the Exam 🎉


💡 Final Tips If You’re Preparing

  • 📚 Pick ONE main course (Ramdayal or Messer—don’t overload)

  • ❓ Do LOTS of practice questions, especially domain 2 (Threats) and domain 4 (Security Operations)

  • 🧠 Focus on understanding, not just remembering ports or acronyms

  • 🧘‍♂️ Stay consistent: 2–3 hours/day is enough if focused


🧭 Final Words

Security+ isn’t about being perfect—it’s about being consistent.
I passed in 22 days by sticking to one plan and grinding through practice questions. You can too.
