🚀 How Ligolo-ng Helped Me Pass the OSCP

2025-08-17
#OSCP  #Ligolo  #Pivoting  #TryHarder 

When preparing for my OSCP, one of the biggest challenges I anticipated was pivoting inside internal networks. The updated exam format almost always requires moving laterally, and doing this manually with sshuttle or proxychains can be messy.

That’s where Ligolo-ng came in. Honestly, learning this tool was one of the best decisions I made during prep—it simplified pivoting so much that I was able to focus more on exploitation instead of fighting with tunneling setups.

🔹 What is Ligolo-ng?

Ligolo-ng is a reverse tunneling tool written in Go. Think of it as a modern version of socat/chisel but faster, more stable, and perfect for OSCP-style pivoting.

  • SOCKS tunneling (so you can proxy Nmap, RDP, SMB, etc.)
  • Local and remote port forwarding
  • Lightweight agent, easy deployment
  • Stable (no random breaks mid-exam 👀)

🔹 Basic Setup

You need two things:

  1. Proxy (server) – runs on your Kali
  2. Agent (client) – runs on the compromised host

Step 1: Start the Proxy

ligolo-proxy -selfcert

Step 2: Run the Agent on Target

./ligolo-agent -connect YOUR_IP:11601

Step 3: Connect via CLI

ligolo-cli
session

🔹 Local & Remote Port Forwarding

Local Port Forwarding (access internal services)

listener_add --addr 127.0.0.1:445 --to 192.168.100.10:445

Remote Port Forwarding (expose Kali service inside)

listener_add --addr 192.168.100.50:80 --to 127.0.0.1:80

🔹 Why Ligolo Helped Me Pass

One of the biggest challenges in long exams like the OSCP is managing time effectively, and that’s where Ligolo really made the difference for me. Instead of fighting with clunky tunneling setups or juggling multiple tools, I had a single, reliable solution that just worked when I needed it to.

Ligolo-ng was fast to set up, stable during use, and flexible enough to handle both SOCKS proxying and simple port forwarding with ease. Its simplicity kept my workflow clean and reduced stress, letting me focus on the important parts—exploitation and documentation. In the end, it wasn’t just a pivoting tool, it was a genuine time-saver and sanity-saver, which helped me cross the finish line.

✅ Final Tip: Don’t wait for exam day. Practice Ligolo in labs so it becomes second nature.
